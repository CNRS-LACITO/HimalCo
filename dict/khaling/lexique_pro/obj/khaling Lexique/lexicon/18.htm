ʣ

ʣA   vt. eat (crops). 1s: ʣʌŋʌ. 1d: ʣɵji. ʔɛ̂n jûː ʣɵki. Now let us have a meal. ŋeçkilo ʔʌusʌdhi ʣɵnɛ mʌttʉ. When we are sick, we have to take medicine.

ʣikt   vt. paint, dye. 1px: ʣʌktu. 1d: ʣiki. ʔuŋʌ kʌ̄m ʣʌktu. I paint the house. bubûm gɵ ɦʌlʌlâm rʌ̄ŋʔɛ ʣʌktu. I dyed the white piece of cloth red.

ʣe   vi. say. 1s: ʣeŋʌ. 1d: ʣeji. ɦebe ʣēːtɛ? What time is it? khɛ̂l mɵ̂l mʉnɛ lagi ghole ɦʌskolo ʣenɛ mʌttʉ. We have to discuss Khaling affairs with many people. ʔʌ̄m ʦɛnʉpɛ ʣe. He speaks nicely. ʔʌ̄mpo joŋbêm ʦɛnʉpɛ ʣe. His jew's harp sounds nice. Voir : ʣet.

ʣett   vt. talk to, read. 1s: ʣettu. 1d: ʣeʦi. lɛ̄mdubi ʔʌ̄mʔɛ ʔûŋ ʔiʣestʌlo ʦhêntʌ. When he called to me on the road, I recognized him. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ bideslʌ ɦɵpɛ brâː sāːphʌm ʣettɛ. He read a letter from abroad. nokʦhoʔɛ ʔusesmɵ ʣettʉ. The shaman calls his master. ʣêi lʉkinʌ go ʦhēi lʉki. We get to know each other by talking.

ʣhit   vi. be wet. ʔʌnɵ̂l ʔʌʦhatʌ mugōːwɛnʌ wɵ̂ːʔɛ ʣhistʌ. As I did not have an umbrella today, I got wet from the rain.

ʣhitt   vt. soak, grow (seedling). 1s: ʣhʌttu. 1d: ʣhiʦi. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ ʔiʣhisti. He made us wet. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ rɵ̂ː rāːru ʣhʌttɛ. He grew the rice seedlings.

ʣhitt   vt. get warm by the fire / get coolness (when one feels too hot); adjust one's temperature to surroundings. 1s: ʣhʌttu. 1d: ʣhiʦi. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ mi ʣhʌttʉ. He gets warm by the fire. ʣhūŋ lʉlo mi ʣhʌ̂nnɛ ʦɛnʉ. When it is cold, it is nice to get warm by the fire. gʌrmi lʉlo ʣhūŋ ʣhʌ̂nnɛ ʦɛnʉ. When it is hot, it is nice to have a cool wind. lonɛmbi tori tēlʔɛ klênsinu mʌnʌ noɔ̄m ʣhʌtnu. In the winter, they rub themselves with oil and bask in the sun.

ʣhint   vt. persuade, incite, encourage. 1px: ʣhʌ̄ndu. 1d: ʣhīːʦi. mʌbʌ̄ŋpɛ kɛ̄mbi ɦʌs ʣhʌ̄inɛ munʉ. It is not good to encourage people to do bad work. ʔûː ʣhōːnɛ ʔok ʔiʣhʌ̄itʌkʌ. He encouraged us to plough the field.

ʣhip   vi. spoil, rot. phɵrsi ʣhiptɛ. The pumpkin rotted. ki ʣhiptɛ. The potato spoiled. sɵ ʣhiptɛ. The meat spoiled.

ʣhim (môː)   vi. be fed up, be bored, drink enough water. kɛ̄m mʉnɛʔɛ ʔʌmôː ʣhîmtɛ. I am fed up of working too much. ʔudinʌi poksɵ kɵnɛʔɛ ʔʌmôː ʣhîmtɛ. I am bored of eating pork every day. ʔumôː ʣhîmtɛ̂m sʌmmʌ mesʔɛ ku tʉ̂ŋtɛ. The buffalo drank water until it was satisfied.

ʣhʉk   vi. jump down, escape. 1s: ʣhûːŋʌ. 1d: ʣhʉki. khespɵ ʣhʉkkhɵstɛ. The thief escaped. nus tɛ̄ːlâm sʌ̄ŋ mɛ̄ːlâm sʌ̄ŋbi ʣʉkkhɵstɛ. The monkey jumped from tree to tree.

ʣhʉr   vi. be sour. 3s: ʣhʉ̂rtɛ. ʦi ʣhʉ̂rtɛnʌ ʔʌ̄mʔɛ mutʉ̂ŋwɛ. As the alcohol was sour, he did not drink it. ʣhʌ̄rpɛ rɛ̄ikhɛmulʌ sɵpɛ phʌ̄l jāŋ. With a tight sieve, one can sift fine flour.

ʣhekt   vt. cut (halfway). 1s: ʣhektu. 1d: ʣheki. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ poɔ̄ndʉʔɛ sʌ̄ŋ ʣhektʉ. He cut the tree.

ʣheŋ   vi. last. 1s: ʣhēːŋʌ. 1d: ʣheŋi. ghole ʣhêŋtɛ. It lasted for a long time. ʔʌnʌ̄m ʔʌmɛ̂mʔɛ pʉtɛ̂m thoɔpru mʌri ʣhêŋtɛ. The girdle that my mother wove years ago lasted a very long time. ʔʌnʌ̄mmîm ɦʌsɦɛm mʌri ʣhêŋtɛnu. People in old days used to live a very long time.

ʣhɛp   vi. be possible. ʔʌnɵ̂l ʣhɛ̂ːpkho ghole kɛ̄m mʉnɛ mʌttʉ. Today, if possible, we have to do a lot of work. ʔʌnɵ̂l khɵlêŋ kɛ̄m mumʉki muʣhɛ̂p. Today we have to do all the work.

ʣhɛm   vi. be lost, disappear, die (formal). thūːnɛmbi bʌ̂i ʣhɛ̂mtɛ. The cow got lost in the forest. thūːnɛmbi ʦɵʦʦɵpo ʔulɵ ʣhɛ̄m. The child is terrified in the forest.

ʣhɵŋ   vt. plough. ʔuŋʌ ʔûː ʣhoŋu. I plough the field. ʔuŋʌ kʌwʌ ʣhoŋutʌnʌ lɛ̄ːstʌ. I waded across the stream.

ʣhɵr   vi. leak, become thin. 3sn: ʣhoɔ̄r. ʔʌ̄m ʔʌ̂mtɛsilo ʔukhîːmɵ ʣhɵ̂rtɛnʌ dhāŋku ʣhistɛ. While he was sleeping, his saliva leaked out and the pillow got wet. ʔʌtēmmîm dʉkʔɛ ʔʌ̄m ʣhɵ̂rtɛ. He became thin because of the hardships which he endured this year. poksɵ ɦʌttɛlo ʔuʦhodowɛ ʣhɵ̂rtɛ When we roasted pork, the fat dripped. ɦal ɦʌlsilo ʔâːppo ʔâmkhi ʣhoɔ̄r. When the temperature increases, the pine resin comes out.
