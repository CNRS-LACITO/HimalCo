ʔi   vi. sulk, become angry. 3s: ʔūːtɛ. ʔʌ̄m sʉ̂ːkʌ ʔūːtɛ. Who is he angry with? ʔipɛ ɦʌskolo brâː mʉnɛ muʦɛnʉ. It is not pleasant to talk to an angry person. mɛ brâːkʌ ʦʌ̄i ʔûŋ ʔiŋʌtʌ. That conversation made me angry.

ʔiŋ   vt. buy. 1s: ʔiŋutʌ. ʔuŋʌ tɵpi ʔiŋutʌ. ʔuŋʌ ʦʌpdem ʔiŋutʌ. I bought a hat.

ʔiŋ-si   vr. buy for oneself. ʔûŋ tɵpi ʔunsiŋʌtʌ. I bought a hat for myself.

ʔipt   vt. put to sleep. 1s: ʔʌptu. 1d: ʔipi. ʦɵʦʦɵ bhʌ̄ŋte ʔʌ̂mnɛ mʌttʉ. One has to put children to sleep gently.

ʔipt-si   vr. sleep. 1px: ʔʌ̂msiŋʌ.

ʔipt   vt. cheat. bʌ̄ŋpɛ saman ʔɛstɛnʌ ʔibîiti molo phoso ʔiʔipiti. He said that it was a good product and gave it to us, but he cheated us.

ʔʉ   vt. wake, take care, save. 1s: ʔʉŋʌ. ʔuŋʌ reskʌp ʦɵʦʦɵ ʔʉ̂ŋtʌ. I took care of an orphan. ɖʌkʈʌrʔɛ mʌ̂nnɛ khɛppɛ ŋeçpɛ ɦʌs ʔʉtɛ. The doctor saved the sick person who was about to die. titi titi ghoɔ̄lpɛ buplaɦɛm bhɵ̄ːlɛ bhɵ̄ːlɛ ʔʉ̂ŋtʌnu. We saved the chicks with difficulty.

ʔu-si   vr. survive. 1px: ʔʉ̂nsiŋʌ. ʔûŋ ŋeçpɛ muŋʌtam bhɵ̄ːlɛ bhɵ̄ːlɛ ʔʉ̂nsiŋʌtʌ. As I was sick, I survived with difficulty.

ʔʉn   vi. fall down (tree). sʌ̄ŋ ʔʉ̄ːstɛ. The tree fell down.

ʔʉnt   vt. fell (tree). ʣhūŋʔɛ sʌ̄ŋ ʔʌ̂ntɛ. The wind broke the tree. leŋaksipo ʔuʣɛʔɛ ʔukhāŋ ʔʌ̂ntɛ. The banana tree broke under the weight of iʦ fruits.

ʔʉm   vt. put in the mouth. 1s: ʔumu. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ ʔukoɔ̄mbi ki ʔʉ̄mdetʉ. He puts a potato in his mouth.

ʔʉl   vt. uproot. 1s: ʔulu. 1d: ʔʉli. gɵ̄ːthɛ jokkilo ɦūːsʌŋ ʔʌ̄lnɛnʌ khoɔ̂nnɛ mʌttʉ. When we move the cowshed, we have to uproot the vertical pole and take it away. ʦɵʦʦɵpo ŋʌlu ɦʌllei ʦhûː jo ʔʌ̄lkinʌkho nuri ŋʌlu bʌ̄ŋpɛ lʌ̄m. If we take out the children teeth when they are loose, the new teeth grow well. sʌ̄ŋbi thânpɛ kaʈi ʣʌmʌrʌʔɛ ʔʌ̄lnɛ mʌttʉ. We have to take out the nails that have been nailed into the wood with a hammer.

ʔe   vi. go to the toilets.

ʔek   vt. tear. 1s: ʔegu. 1d: ʔeki. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ gɵ prɛ̂ː ʔêːktɛ. He tore the clothes.

ʔen   vi. be left unfinished. ʔʌmiski khʌ̂mpɛ jûː ʔēːstɛ. The food cooked yesterday was left unfinished.

ʔent   vt. leave unfinished. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ jûː ʔêntɛ. He left the food unfinished.

ʔent-si   vr. leave for later (food). disɛ̂ː po lagi jûː ʔēisinɛ mʌttʉ. We have to leave some food for tomorrow.

ʔemt   vt. heat. 3sn: ʔēmdʉ. mes noɔ̄mʔɛ mʌri ʔêmtɛ. The sun overheated the buffalo. sɵbem mibi ʔēmnɛ mʌttʉ. We have to warm the bread in the fire. miʔɛ dhuma ʔiʔemʌtʌ. The fire warmed me up.

ʔel   vi. overflow. 3sn: ʔēl. ku ʔêltɛnʌ mɵptɛ. The water overflowed and spilled over. dʉt bhlʌttɛnʌ ʔêltɛ. The milk boiled and overflowed.

ʔɛt   vi-t. say. 1px: ʔɛ̂iŋʌ. ʔʌ̄mɦɛmʔɛ bʌʣar khoɔçkʌ ʔɛstɛnu. They said ``We're going to the market".

ʔɛt-si   vr. ʔɵdi mʉto ʔɛ̂nsi. We will be careful of what we say. mɛʔo ʔɛ̂ntɛsi Did I say that?

ʔɛnt   vt. bend. 1px: ʔɛ̄ndu. ʔuŋʌ tēr ʔɛ̄ndu. I bend the bamboo.

ʔɛnt-si   vr. look to the side, dodge. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ ʔɛ̄isisʌ sêntɛ. He looked from the side (with his upper body bent in half).

ʔɛm   vt. expel (spirits). nokʦhoʔɛ bhut pret ʔɛ̂mtɛnu. The shaman expelled the bad spirits. ʔɛ̄mpɛ wārbʌlʌŋ pʉtɛ. He wove a basket with a wide funnel-shaped opening.

ʔɛl   vt. pull forward. 1px: ʔɛlu. kunabi tɵ̂npɛ kʉ̂r kʌ̄r belabi ʔɛ̄lnɛ mʌttʉ. When taking a load that was laid on a terrace wall, one has to pull it forward. ʔʌ̄msuʔɛ ghoɔ̄lpɛ lūŋ gʌ̄lʔɛ ʔɛ̂ltɛsu. They lifted the boulder with a crowbar. ʔʌsɛ̂l dhuktʌlo sēndi ʔɛ̂ltɛ. My nail came off when I bumped my foot.

ʔɵk   vi. crow. 3s: ʔɵktɛ. koklʌp ʔudiʦʦɛ ʔɵktɛ. The rooster crowed in the morning.

ʔɵkt   vt. bend (wood). noɔ̄mʔɛ ghɵ̂ŋtɛnʌ sʌ̄ŋ ʔoktɛ. The wood bent as the sun dried it.

ʔɵŋ   vt. put (one's head) halfway into a window or door. laskʌlʌ ʔudhōŋ ʔɵ̂ŋtɛ. He put his head halfway into the door.

ʔɵŋ-si   vr. get warm by the fire. mi phɛ̄rbi ʔûŋ ʔondensiŋʌtʌ I sat down by the fire.

ʔɵt   vi. come back, return. 1px: ʔoɔ̂iŋʌ. ʔûŋ jɛlʌkhoɔmkʌ dēlkʌm ʔɵstʌ. I returned to my village from Kathmandu. ʔik kāːkukʌ phʌ̂lle ʔoɔçtiki. We came back to Phuleli from Kanku.

ʔɵtt   vt. bring back. ʔuŋʌ ʔûː ʣhoŋulo goru ʔoɔttu. When I plough fields, I make the ox turn around.

ʔɵtt (sɛt)   take revenge. jakki brâːbi sɛt ʔoɔnnɛ munʉ. It is not good to take revenge for small matters.

ʔɵtt-si   vr. bring something back for oneself. ʔuŋ bɛjoŋ ʔoɔ̂nsiŋʌtʌ. I brought back the comb for myself (I left other things in some place but I brought back the comb).

ʔɵnt   vt. pronounce ritual words. 1px: ʔoɔ̄ndu. negi nokʦhoʔɛ negi ʔoɔ̂ntɛ. The nagi shaman pronounced ritual words pertaining to the nagi. boɔ̄l nokʦhoʔɛ mʌ̂n tʌ̄mrʌ ʔoɔ̂ntɛ. The priest who performs death rituals pronounced ritual words pertaining to the funeral.

ʔɵp   vt. shoot. 1s: ʔobu. 1d: ʔɵpi. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ lʌphu ʔɵ̂ːptɛnʌ bhîr sêːtɛ. He killed the deer by shooting with his bow.

ʔɵp-si   vr. shoot oneself. lʌphuʔɛ ʔoɔ̂msiŋʌtʌ. I shot myself with an arrow.

ʔɵpt   vt-i. rise (sun). noɔ̄m ʔoɔptɛ. The sun rose.

ʔɵpt   vt-i. jump. nɛmdʉrêm ʔoɔptɛ. The locust jumped. ʦʌkʌpɛ ʔoɔpkhʌtɛ. The flea jumped.

ʔɵmt   vt-i. ripen (rice, wheat). lūːʣʌ ʔoɔ̂mtɛ. The millet ripened. ʣhes ʔoɔ̂mtɛ. Winter arrived (the highland ripened).

ʔɵr   vt. break (the cob of the corn in such a way that the stalks remain in place). 1s: ʔoru. 1d: ʔɵri. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ mʌkʌi ʔɵ̂rtɛ. He broke the cob of the corn.

ʔɵrt   vt. croak. ʔuŋʌ mʌkʌi muʔoɔ̂rsʌtwʌlo ʔʌ̄m ʔuʦîlʔɛ ʔoɔ̂rtɛ When I did not break off the ears of corn for him, he became very angry. jʉnɛmbi ʔusenɛ bhoro ʔoɔ̂rnu. Frogs croak at night in the spring. jûː kɛpbi ku ʔoɔ̂rtɛ. The water bubbled up in the cooking pot.

ʔɵl   vt. reimburse. 1px: ʔolu. nôː ʣɵkîm pʌʦhi ʔoɔ̄lnɛ mʌttʉ. After we take a loan, we have to reimburse it. ʔʌ̄mʔɛ phʌ̄l jûː mujɛtwɛ wôːne mēːnʌ ʔɵ̂lsʉtɛ.

ʔɵl   vt. spit out the mouth. 1px: ʔolu.
